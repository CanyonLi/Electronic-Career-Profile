Independent Developer
@Desmos Studio

This project showcases the integration of mathematical modeling, real-world applications, and educational tools using the power of Desmos. Each virtual model highlights the relevance of mathematics, physics, and chemistry through dynamic visualizations and computational solutions. Below is an organized and comprehensive summary of the project's components:

Real-World Applications

  1. EV Future Market Share
    • Description: Uses Python and machine learning to perform statistical analysis and data regression, predicting the market share of electric vehicles through 2035.
    • Relevance: Highlights the intersection of data science, sustainability, and market dynamics.
  2. Cycloid Graph for Brachistochrone
    • Description: Visualizes and explains one of the most classic and time-consuming problems in the history of mathematics and physics: the Brachistochrone curve.
    • Relevance: Demonstrates the elegance of cycloidal curves in solving real-world problems of minimal time travel under gravity.
    • Link: Brachistochrone Curve
  3. Chemistry Calculator
    • Description: Effortlessly balances chemical equations, calculates molar ratios, and determines limiting reactants in multi-step reactions.
    • Relevance: Bridges the gap between abstract chemistry concepts and computational precision for practical applications.

AP Calculus Theorem Virtual Models (9 models)

  • 1. The Squeeze Theorem: Visualizes sin(θ)/θ limit behavior as θ approaches 0.
  • 2. Tangent Line Approximation: Demonstrates linear approximation of curves using tangents.
  • 3. Area of a Region Between Two Curves: Calculates and visualizes the area enclosed by two curves.
  • 4. Volume: The Disk & Washer Method: Explains volumetric calculations using the disk and washer method for rotational solids.
  • 5. Volume: The Shell Method: Illustrates volume calculation using cylindrical shells.
  • 6. Derivatives, Arc Length, Surface Areas for Parametric Equations: Explores derivatives, arc lengths, and surface areas derived from parametric equations.
  • 7. Parametric Equations Powerful Techniques: Showcases advanced modeling techniques for parametric equations.
  • 8. nth Complex Roots (Polar): Visualizes and computes nth roots of complex numbers using polar coordinates.
  • 9. Taylor Polynomial for f(x)=sin(x): Expands sin(x) using Taylor series and visualizes convergence.

  • - Formulated recommendations for next-generation PEM fuel cell design and optimization in clean energy applications.

Key Skills & Accomplishments

  • Statistical Modeling: Applied regression analysis, hypothesis testing, and ANOVA to interpret efficiency trends and validate hypotheses.
  • Technical Proficiency: Set up complex circuits with ammeters, voltmeters, variable resistors, and custom electronics for real-time monitoring.
